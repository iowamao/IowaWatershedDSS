Iowa Culverts

    Culvert design is based on a variety of data sources (time series, statistical analyses, maps, and other site-specific characteristics) that are typically stored in various formats in multiple data provider repositories. In the existing design workflow, culvert information are assembled manually, which is time consuming and inefficient, and post-construction data are not utilized in culvert design due to data Interoperability issues. In addition, sedimentation at multi-barrel culverts has become a widespread problem in the US Midwest as a direct consequence of the changes in land use and climate. Culvert designs are preceded by analyses that take into consideration hydrologic, hydraulic, and geomorphological conditions at the construction site. Less attention is typically given to a thorough assessment of the potential for sedimentation within and in the culvert vicinity. This is unfortunate as both inspection and sediment cleaning at culvert are expensive, labor intensive, in many occasions and sites need to be repeated.

    The prototype geo-portal “Iowa Culvert” presented in this poster is designed for Iowa Department of Transportation to achieve: i) automated culvert designs and data integration; ii) culvert sediment mitigation design; and, iii) digital culvert inspection and monitoring; The platform is a continuously updated resource on culvert life cycle that plays several roles: a) informs and facilitate the design of other culverts in the area; b) serves as a repository that can be mined to infer aspects of current designs in relationship with the location of the culvert in diverse geographic areas. It integrates: i) watershed characteristics (river network, hydrologic and soil information for the drainage areas, streamflow statistics at gaged and ungagged sites); ii) culvert database for the region; iii) hydraulic model for sizing culverts; iv) monitoring data; and v) pre- and post-construction culvert data . Various means for enabling designers and managing personnel to query and visualize these multiple resources. The platform is built using open source technologies to make the system light-weighted, low cost and flexible.

The data provided on this app is for scientific research and academic purposes only.
